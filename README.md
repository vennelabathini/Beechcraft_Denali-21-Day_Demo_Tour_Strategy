A competition-grade Tableau strategy dashboard built for the OSU Datathon, designing a data-driven go-to-market plan to launch the Beechcraft Denali turboprop (Textron Aviation). It models a 40-stop, 21-day demo tour optimized to reach the highest-value competitor-owner prospects, paired with a full cost-and-ROI business case — all on a single, decision-ready canvas.

🧭 Dashboard Walkthrough

The report is a single strategy canvas: a KPI header strip up top, then panels that answer three questions in sequence — where do we go, who do we target, and does the math work?

📊 KPI Header Strip

Seven headline metrics set up the entire pitch: the Denali's current market share (0 of 3,195, vs. PC-12, TBM, Piper M-Series, and Epic), the tour's scale (40 stops, ~3 airports/day, 2.5-hr demo windows), its reach (3,372 prospects at those airports and 445 direct competitor-turboprop owners), and its economics ($40,724 total cost, $707,500 five-year savings vs. the PC-12, and a 19,533% ROI on a single sale).

🗺️ The Demo Tour Route

A U.S. map plots all 40 numbered stops — from KBFI in Seattle down to KTMB in Miami and across to KSAT in Texas — linked by a red dashed route line that traces the optimized 21-day path. A companion "Target Accounts by Zone" view splits prospects across West, Central, and East sales zones, confirming the route reaches high-value accounts in every region rather than clustering in one.

🎯 The Market Opportunity

Four panels size and qualify the prize. A competitor market-share pie shows who owns the turboprop space today — Pilatus (39.70%), Daher (31.36%), and Piper (24.59%). A fleet-age bar reveals the replacement window: 934 aircraft sit in the 15–25-year bucket, prime for upgrade. A market-opportunity funnel narrows 10,580 aircraft owners down to 154 "golden leads," and a buyer-profile pie confirms 82.88% are owner-operators — the decision-makers who actually sign.

💰 The Business Case (Cost & ROI)

The numbers that close the argument. A tour-cost breakdown derives the $40,724 figure transparently — (8,678 nm × $2.89/nm) + (21 days × $745/day) — against a conservative revenue estimate of $39.975M (just five sales at $7.995M each). The ROI works out to a 196× return (19,533%) on a single sale. An operating-cost comparison anchors the pitch: the Denali runs at ~$755/hr, well under the King Air ($1,371), PC-12, and TBM 960 ($1,038 each) — the cost advantage the whole strategy is built around.

💡 Key Insights


The Denali's standout selling point is cost: ~$755/hr to operate vs. King Air ($1,371) and PC-12/TBM ($1,038) — the analysis builds the entire pitch around this advantage.
The biggest opportunity is aging competitor fleets (15–25 years) — 934 accounts entering their natural replacement window.
A funnel from 10,580 aircraft owners down to 154 "golden leads" focuses the tour on the highest-probability buyers (82.88% owner-operators).
The route is geographically optimized across West, Central, and East zones to maximize prospects reached per travel dollar.
The ROI case is decisive — a ~$40K tour against a conservative ~$40M revenue opportunity makes the demo tour an easy yes.
